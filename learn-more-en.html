Knowledge is Your First Step Towards Safety

The more you know, the more capable you become of protecting yourself and making the right decisions.

What is Breast Cancer?

Simply put, breast cancer is the abnormal and uncontrolled growth of breast cells. These cells can form a mass or a lump. Most breast lumps are not cancerous (they are benign), but it is essential to have any new change examined by a specialist to be sure.

Common Symptoms and Warning Signs

Factors That May Increase Risk

Some factors may increase the risk of developing the disease, but having them does not mean you will definitely get it. The most important factors include:

  • Being a woman: Breast cancer is much more common in women.
  • Increasing age: The risk increases as you get older, especially after 50.
  • Family history: Having a first-degree relative (mother, sister, daughter) who had the disease.
  • Genetic factors: Inherited genetic mutations like BRCA1 and BRCA2.
  • Lifestyle: Obesity, lack of physical activity, and smoking may increase the risk.
Very Important Note

Many women who get breast cancer have no known risk factors. Therefore, awareness and regular check-ups are essential for everyone.
