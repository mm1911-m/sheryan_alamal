Your Step-by-Step Guide to Self-Exam

5 minutes a month can make a huge difference. Learn how to be aware of your body.

Best time for the exam: A few days after your period ends, when your breasts are least likely to be swollen.

Part 1: Visual Exam in Front of a Mirror

1

Stand with Hands on Hips

Stand in front of a mirror with your shoulders straight and your arms on your hips. Look for any changes in size, shape, color, or any visible distortion or swelling.

2

Raise Your Arms Up

Raise your arms above your head. Look for the same changes again from a different angle, and pay attention to any changes that appear with this movement.

Part 2: Manual Exam While Lying Down

3

Lie Down and Feel

Lie on your back. Place a pillow under your right shoulder. Use your left hand to examine your right breast. Use the pads of your three middle fingers in a circular motion with light, medium, and firm pressure, covering the entire breast area from your collarbone to the top of your abdomen, and from your armpit to your cleavage.

4

Repeat on the Other Side

Move the pillow under your left shoulder and use your right hand to examine your left breast in the same circular pattern, making sure to cover the entire breast and armpit area.

What if you find something?

Don't panic! Most changes women notice are benign and not cancerous. The goal of the exam is to know your body's normal state. If you notice any new lump or a persistent change, make an appointment with your doctor for reassurance. You are taking the right step.
